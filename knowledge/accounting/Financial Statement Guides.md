# Financial Statement Analysis Guide

---

## How to Read a Trial Balance

A Trial Balance lists all ledger accounts with their debit or credit balances.

**Golden Rules:**
- Total Debits must = Total Credits (basic double-entry check)
- If Trial Balance doesn't tally → accounting error exists

**What each side means:**
| Account Type | Normal Balance | Debit means | Credit means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Assets | Debit | Increase | Decrease |
| Liabilities | Credit | Decrease | Increase |
| Capital/Equity | Credit | Decrease | Increase |
| Income/Revenue | Credit | Decrease | Increase |
| Expenses | Debit | Increase | Decrease |

## How to Read a Balance Sheet

**Three questions to answer:**
1. Is the business solvent? (Assets > Liabilities)
2. Can it pay its short-term dues? (Current Assets > Current Liabilities)
3. Is it funded by debt or equity? (Debt-Equity ratio)

**Red flags in Balance Sheet:**
- Negative net worth (accumulated losses exceed capital)
- Current liabilities > Current assets (liquidity crisis)
- Very high debtors with no provisions (bad debts risk)
- Loans from directors increasing every year (cash being infused to keep business running)
- Fixed assets fully depreciated but still in use (need asset replacement plan)
- Very old creditors outstanding (goods taken but not paid)

## How to Read a P&L

**Three questions to answer:**
1. Is the business profitable? (Revenue > Expenses)
2. Is gross margin healthy? (Industry benchmark comparison)
3. Is it improving or declining? (Year over year comparison)

**Red flags in P&L:**
- Gross profit declining while revenue growing (raw material cost rising)
- Expenses growing faster than revenue (cost control issue)
- Other income > Operating profit (business not self-sustaining)
- Large professional fees with no explanation (possible diversion of funds)
- Salary not increasing while business grows (possible related party payments elsewhere)
- No advertising expense in growing company (grey market sales?)

## Cash Flow Statement (AS-3)

Three sections:

**Operating Activities:**
- Net profit
- Add back: Depreciation, amortization (non-cash)
- Changes in working capital: increase in debtors = cash outflow
- Tax paid

**Investing Activities:**
- Purchase of fixed assets (outflow)
- Sale of fixed assets (inflow)
- Investments made/sold

**Financing Activities:**
- Loans taken (inflow)
- Loans repaid (outflow)
- Capital introduced (inflow)
- Drawings/dividends (outflow)

**Key insight:** A company can show profit but negative operating cash flow — this means it's profitable on paper but not collecting cash from customers.

## Working Capital Analysis

**Working Capital = Current Assets − Current Liabilities**

**Working Capital Cycle:**
```
Cash → Raw Material → WIP → Finished Goods → Debtors → Cash
```

**Calculating Working Capital Days:**
- Inventory days = (Stock / COGS) × 365
- Debtor days = (Debtors / Revenue) × 365
- Creditor days = (Creditors / Purchases) × 365
- Working Capital Days = Inventory days + Debtor days − Creditor days

**Shorter working capital cycle = healthier cash flow**

## Fund Flow Statement

Shows movement of funds (long-term capital changes):

**Sources of Funds:**
- Profit from operations
- Long-term loans taken
- Capital introduced
- Sale of fixed assets

**Application of Funds:**
- Loss from operations
- Loan repayments
- Drawings/dividends
- Purchase of fixed assets

**Sources = Application** (always balances)

## Audit Red Flags by Account

### Bank Account Red Flags
- Cash deposits in round figures multiple times (possible unreported income)
- Large transfers to director's personal account
- Bank balance in books ≠ bank statement (unreconciled items > 30 days)
- Multiple bank accounts not disclosed

### Debtors Red Flags
- Debtor outstanding > 1 year with no provision (bad debt risk)
- Debtor balance increasing while sales flat
- Debtors with credit balance (customer overpaid or goods returned not accounted)
- Single large debtor > 50% of total debtors (concentration risk)

### Creditors Red Flags
- Creditor outstanding > 2 years (possible bogus creditor or income suppression)
- Creditor with debit balance (overpayment or return not booked)
- Creditors reducing while purchases increasing (cash purchases off-books?)

### Stock Red Flags
- Stock increasing while sales declining (slow moving / obsolete stock)
- Gross margin declining (stock being taken out without invoicing?)
- No physical stock count for 2+ years
- Stock written off without management approval

### Capital Account Red Flags
- Large cash introduced as capital (source of funds?)
- Drawings more than profits (business being drained)
- Capital reducing year on year without drawings (continuous losses)
