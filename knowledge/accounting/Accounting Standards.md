# Accounting Standards — Balance Sheet & P&L Structure

---

## Balance Sheet Structure (Schedule III — Companies Act 2013)

### EQUITY AND LIABILITIES

**1. Shareholders' Funds**
- Share Capital
- Reserves and Surplus
  - General Reserve
  - Securities Premium
  - Retained Earnings (P&L balance)
  - Capital Reserve

**2. Non-Current Liabilities**
- Long-term Borrowings (loans > 1 year)
- Deferred Tax Liabilities (net)
- Long-term Provisions (gratuity, leave encashment)

**3. Current Liabilities**
- Short-term Borrowings (bank OD, loans < 1 year)
- Trade Payables (creditors)
- Other Current Liabilities
  - Advance from customers
  - Statutory dues (TDS payable, GST payable, PT payable, PF payable)
  - Expenses payable (accrued expenses)
- Short-term Provisions (proposed dividend, short-term gratuity)

---

### ASSETS

**1. Non-Current Assets**
- Fixed Assets
  - Tangible Assets (land, building, plant, machinery, vehicles, furniture, computers)
  - Intangible Assets (goodwill, patents, software)
  - Capital Work in Progress (assets under construction)
- Non-Current Investments
- Long-term Loans and Advances (deposits, advance tax paid)
- Other Non-Current Assets

**2. Current Assets**
- Current Investments
- Inventories (stock)
- Trade Receivables (debtors)
- Cash and Cash Equivalents
  - Cash in hand
  - Balances with banks
  - Cheques in hand
- Short-term Loans and Advances
  - Advance to suppliers
  - Staff advances
  - Prepaid expenses
  - TDS receivable
  - GST receivable (ITC)
- Other Current Assets

---

## P&L Structure (Schedule III)

### Revenue from Operations
- Sales of goods
- Sales of services
- Other operating income (scrap, export incentives)

### Other Income
- Interest income
- Dividend income
- Profit on sale of assets
- Miscellaneous income

### Total Revenue = Revenue from Operations + Other Income

---

### Expenses

**Cost of Materials Consumed**
- Opening stock of raw material + Purchases − Closing stock

**Purchases of Stock-in-Trade** (for trading companies)

**Changes in Inventories**
- Opening stock − Closing stock (of WIP and finished goods)
- If closing > opening → negative (reduces expenses)
- If closing < opening → positive (increases expenses)

**Employee Benefit Expenses**
- Salaries and wages
- PF contribution (employer's share)
- ESI contribution
- Gratuity expense
- Staff welfare

**Finance Costs**
- Interest on loans
- Bank charges
- Processing fees

**Depreciation and Amortisation**

**Other Expenses**
- Rent
- Electricity
- Professional fees
- Repairs and maintenance
- Advertisement
- Travelling
- Communication
- Printing and stationery
- Insurance
- Audit fees
- Miscellaneous

- Furniture and Fixtures

## Key Financial Ratios — What They Mean

| Ratio | Formula | Good Range | What It Tells |
|---|---|---|---|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 – 2.0 | Short-term liquidity |
| Quick Ratio | (CA − Stock) / CL | > 1.0 | Immediate liquidity |
| Debt-Equity Ratio | Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity | < 2.0 | Financial leverage |
| Gross Profit % | Gross Profit / Revenue × 100 | Industry-specific | Operating efficiency |
| Net Profit % | Net Profit / Revenue × 100 | Industry-specific | Overall profitability |
| Inventory Turnover | COGS / Average Inventory | Higher = better | Stock management |
| Debtor Days | (Debtors / Revenue) × 365 | Lower = better | Collection efficiency |
| Creditor Days | (Creditors / Purchases) × 365 | Higher = better | Payment terms |
| Interest Coverage | EBIT / Interest | > 3.0 | Ability to pay interest |
