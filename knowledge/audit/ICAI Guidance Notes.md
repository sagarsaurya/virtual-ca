# ICAI Guidance Notes — Auditing Standards Summary

**Source:** Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI)

---

## Standards on Auditing (SA) — Complete List

### SA 200 Series — General Principles

**SA 200 — Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor**
- Auditor's objective: Express opinion on financial statements
- Financial statements must: be prepared per applicable framework, give true and fair view
- Auditor must maintain: professional skepticism, professional judgment, ethical requirements

**SA 210 — Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements**
- Engagement letter must be signed before audit begins
- Contains: scope, responsibilities, fees, reporting format

**SA 220 — Quality Control for an Audit**
- Audit firm must have quality control policies
- Engagement partner responsible for overall quality

**SA 230 — Audit Documentation**
- Working papers must support audit opinion
- Retained for minimum 7 years
- Must include: nature, timing, extent of procedures; results; conclusions

**SA 240 — Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud**
- Auditor responsible for detecting material misstatements due to fraud
- Must maintain professional skepticism throughout
- Report fraud to management/TCWG/regulators as applicable

**SA 250 — Laws and Regulations**
- Auditor must consider compliance with laws/regulations
- Non-compliance that has material effect must be reported

---

### SA 300 Series — Risk Assessment

**SA 300 — Planning an Audit**
- Audit must be planned to reduce risk to acceptably low level
- Develop overall audit strategy and detailed audit plan

**SA 315 — Identifying and Assessing Risks**
- Understand entity and its environment
- Understand internal controls
- Identify risks of material misstatement (RMM)
- Assess RMM at: financial statement level + assertion level

**SA 320 — Materiality in Planning and Performing**
- Materiality = threshold above which misstatements matter to users
- Typical benchmarks: 5% of profit before tax, 0.5-1% of revenue, 1-2% of total assets
- Performance materiality: lower than overall materiality

**SA 330 — Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks**
- Overall responses to financial statement level risks
- Audit procedures at assertion level: tests of controls + substantive procedures

---

### SA 400 Series — Audit Evidence

**SA 402 — Service Organizations**
- When client uses third-party service (payroll processor, cloud software)
- Obtain Type 1 or Type 2 report from service auditor

**SA 450 — Evaluation of Misstatements**
- Accumulate misstatements found during audit
- Determine if individually or collectively material
- Request management to correct material misstatements

---

### SA 500 Series — Audit Evidence

**SA 500 — Audit Evidence**
- Evidence must be sufficient (quantity) and appropriate (quality)
- Sources: inspection, observation, inquiry, confirmation, recalculation, analytical procedures

**SA 501 — Specific Considerations — Inventory**
- Attend physical stocktaking (or alternative if impracticable)
- Confirm consignment stock separately

**SA 505 — External Confirmations**
- Confirm balances with: banks, debtors, creditors, lawyers
- Negative vs positive confirmations — positive preferred

**SA 510 — Initial Audit Engagements — Opening Balances**
- Verify opening balances when auditing for first time
- Obtain prior auditor's working papers if possible

**SA 520 — Analytical Procedures**
- Compare: current year vs prior year, actual vs budget, ratios
- Investigate significant variations

**SA 530 — Audit Sampling**
- Random or systematic sampling for large populations
- Document basis for sample size

**SA 540 — Accounting Estimates**
- Verify management's estimates for: depreciation, provisions, fair values
- Assess whether estimates are reasonable

**SA 550 — Related Parties**
- Identify all related parties and transactions
- Verify: disclosed in notes to accounts as required by AS-18

**SA 560 — Subsequent Events**
- Events after balance sheet date but before audit report
- Adjusting events: must be incorporated in accounts
- Non-adjusting events: disclose in notes

**SA 570 — Going Concern**
- Assess if business can continue for at least 12 months
- Red flags: recurring losses, negative working capital, loan defaults, major litigation

**SA 580 — Written Representations**
- Management representation letter required from MD/CFO
- Confirms: completeness of information, no fraud, going concern

---

### SA 600 Series — Using Others' Work

**SA 610 — Internal Audit**
- External auditor can use internal audit work
- Must assess competence and objectivity of internal audit function

**SA 620 — Expert's Work**
- When specialist expertise needed (property valuer, actuary)
- Assess expert's competence and objectivity

---

### SA 700 Series — Audit Conclusions

**SA 700 — Forming and Expressing Opinion**
- Unmodified opinion: "True and fair view"
- Report to include: addressee, opinion, basis, management's responsibility, auditor's responsibility

**SA 705 — Modifications to Opinion**
| Situation | Type of Opinion |
|---|---|
| Material but not pervasive | Qualified opinion |
| Material and pervasive | Adverse opinion |
| Unable to obtain sufficient evidence — material not pervasive | Qualified — except for |
| Unable to obtain sufficient evidence — material and pervasive | Disclaimer of opinion |

**SA 706 — Emphasis of Matter / Other Matter Paragraphs**
- Emphasis of Matter: Draw attention to matter already disclosed in accounts (going concern, litigation)
- Other Matter: Draw attention to matter not in accounts but relevant to users

**SA 720 — Other Information**
- Auditor must read other information in annual report
- Ensure no material inconsistency with financial statements

## Key Audit Assertions

For each account balance and transaction, auditor verifies:

| Assertion | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Existence** | Asset/liability exists at reporting date |
| **Completeness** | All transactions recorded |
| **Rights & Obligations** | Entity has rights to assets, obligations for liabilities |
| **Valuation & Allocation** | Recorded at appropriate amounts |
| **Cutoff** | Transactions in correct period |
| **Classification** | Recorded in correct account |
| **Occurrence** | Transactions actually occurred |
| **Accuracy** | Transactions recorded at correct amounts |
| **Presentation** | Disclosed appropriately |

## Audit Documentation — Minimum Required

1. Engagement letter
2. Audit plan and risk assessment
3. Working trial balance
4. Lead schedules for each major account
5. Bank reconciliation statements
6. Debtors confirmation and ageing
7. Creditors confirmation and ageing
8. Inventory count sheets / certificates
9. Fixed asset register
10. Loan confirmations
11. Management representation letter
12. Tax computation workings
13. Final signed financial statements
14. Signed audit report
