# Standards on Auditing — Quick Reference for VirtualCA

---

## What Constitutes a "True and Fair" Financial Statement?

A financial statement gives a **true and fair view** when:
1. It complies with applicable accounting standards (AS-1 to AS-29 for non-Ind AS companies)
2. All material items are disclosed
3. No material misstatements exist
4. Accounting policies are appropriate and consistently applied

---

## Materiality — Practical Thresholds

| Base | Materiality % | Example (₹1 crore revenue) |
|---|---|---|
| Profit before tax | 5-10% | ₹5,000–₹10,000 on ₹1 lakh profit |
| Revenue | 0.5-1% | ₹50,000–₹1,00,000 |
| Total assets | 1-2% | ₹1,00,000–₹2,00,000 |
| Net assets | 1-2% | Depends on equity |

**Rule of thumb:** An error is material if knowing it would change a user's decision.

## Key Accounting Standards (AS) — Small Business Focus

### AS-1 — Disclosure of Accounting Policies
- All significant accounting policies must be disclosed in notes
- Must state: basis of preparation (historical cost), revenue recognition method, depreciation method, inventory valuation

### AS-2 — Valuation of Inventories
- Value at: cost or net realisable value (NRV), whichever lower
- Cost methods allowed: FIFO or Weighted Average
- LIFO NOT permitted under Indian GAAP
- Cost includes: purchase price + conversion costs + other costs to bring to present location

### AS-5 — Net Profit or Loss for the Period
- Prior period items: must be disclosed separately
- Extraordinary items: must be disclosed separately
- Changes in accounting estimates: disclosed in notes

### AS-6 — WITHDRAWN (Depreciation Accounting)
- Now covered by Schedule II of Companies Act 2013
- For non-corporate entities: follow Income Tax Act rates or AS-10

### AS-9 — Revenue Recognition
- Recognize revenue when: significant risks/rewards transferred to buyer
- For services: proportionate completion method
- Do NOT recognize: when collectability is uncertain, when significant obligations remain

### AS-10 — Property, Plant and Equipment
- Capitalize: purchase price + import duties + taxes + directly attributable costs
- Do NOT capitalize: administrative overheads, training costs, startup losses
- Revaluation: allowed, but entire class must be revalued
- Componentization: significant components depreciated separately

### AS-13 — Accounting for Investments
- Current investments: lower of cost and fair value
- Long-term investments: cost less permanent diminution
- Investment in subsidiaries: not consolidated in standalone (disclose)

### AS-15 — Employee Benefits
- Short-term: salary, bonus, leave — accrue as expense
- Post-employment: PF (defined contribution), gratuity (defined benefit)
- Gratuity: actuarial valuation required, fund with LIC or trust

### AS-16 — Borrowing Costs
- Capitalize borrowing costs for qualifying assets (takes substantial time to get ready)
- All other borrowing costs → expense in P&L

### AS-17 — Segment Reporting
- Required if enterprise is listed OR turnover > ₹50 crore
- Identify primary and secondary segments

### AS-18 — Related Party Disclosures
- Related parties: subsidiaries, associates, KMP, relatives of KMP, entities with common control
- Must disclose: nature of relationship, type of transactions, amounts, outstanding balances
- **This is frequently missed in small business audits**

### AS-19 — Leases
- Finance lease: capitalize asset + liability (economic ownership)
- Operating lease: expense as incurred
- **Post Ind AS: all leases capitalized — but for non-Ind AS companies, AS-19 applies**

### AS-22 — Deferred Tax
- Timing differences between book profit and taxable profit
- Deferred tax liability: book profit > taxable profit (will pay more tax in future)
- Deferred tax asset: taxable profit > book profit (will save tax in future)
- DTA recognized only if virtually certain of future profits

### AS-26 — Intangible Assets
- Capitalize only if: identifiable, control over resource, expected future benefits
- Amortize over useful life (max 10 years unless justified)
- Research costs: expense; Development costs: may capitalize if 6 criteria met
- Internally generated goodwill: NEVER capitalize

### AS-28 — Impairment of Assets
- Test for impairment if indicators exist (loss-making, market value decline, obsolescence)
- Recoverable amount = higher of NRV and Value in Use
- Impairment loss = Carrying amount − Recoverable amount
- Reverse if conditions improve (except goodwill)

### AS-29 — Provisions, Contingent Liabilities, Contingent Assets
- Provision: probable obligation, amount reliably estimable → create provision
- Contingent liability: possible obligation → disclose in notes, do NOT provide
- Contingent asset: NEVER recognize, disclose only if virtually certain

## Common Accounting Errors Found in Audits

| Error | Applicable AS | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue recognized on advance receipt | AS-9 | Overstated revenue |
| Repairs charged to capital | AS-10 | Understated P&L, overstated assets |
| Capital expenditure charged to revenue | AS-10 | Understated assets, inflated expenses |
| Closing stock undervalued | AS-2 | Understated profit |
| No provision for doubtful debts | AS-29 | Overstated assets |
| Related party transactions not disclosed | AS-18 | Non-compliance, potential regulatory issue |
| Deferred tax not computed | AS-22 | Misstated tax expense |
| Lease not classified correctly | AS-19 | Wrong assets/liabilities |
| Prior period expenses in current year | AS-5 | Distorted current year profit |
| Borrowing cost not capitalized | AS-16 | Understated assets, inflated expenses |
