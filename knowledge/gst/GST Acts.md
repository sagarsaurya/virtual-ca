# GST Acts — Key Sections for Audit & Compliance

**Source:** Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017 (CBIC)
**Applicable from:** 1 July 2017
**Last updated in knowledge base:** June 2026

---

## Structure of GST Law

| Act | Purpose |
|---|---|
| CGST Act 2017 | Central GST — levied by Central Government |
| SGST Act 2017 | State GST — levied by State Government |
| IGST Act 2017 | Integrated GST — interstate transactions |
| UTGST Act 2017 | Union Territory GST |
| GST Compensation Act | Compensates states for revenue loss |

## Key Sections — CGST Act 2017

### Section 2 — Definitions
- **Aggregate Turnover**: Total value of all taxable + exempt + export supplies
- **Input Tax Credit (ITC)**: Tax paid on inputs, claimable against output tax liability
- **Taxable Person**: Anyone registered or required to be registered under GST
- **Composite Supply**: Two or more supplies bundled together, one being principal supply
- **Mixed Supply**: Two or more supplies not naturally bundled

### Section 9 — Levy and Collection
- CGST levied on intra-state supply of goods/services
- Rate: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% depending on HSN/SAC code
- Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM): Buyer pays GST instead of supplier

### Section 10 — Composition Scheme
- Turnover limit: ₹1.5 crore (goods), ₹50 lakh (services)
- Composition rate: 1% (traders), 2% (manufacturers), 5% (restaurants)
- Cannot claim ITC under composition
- Cannot make interstate supplies

### Section 16 — Input Tax Credit (ITC) Eligibility
**4 conditions to claim ITC:**
1. Must have tax invoice or debit note
2. Must have received goods/services
3. Tax must have been paid by supplier to government
4. Must have filed GST return

**ITC BLOCKED on (Section 17(5)):**
- Motor vehicles (unless used for resale/transport)
- Food, beverages, outdoor catering
- Club, health, fitness centre membership
- Works contract services for immovable property
- Personal consumption items

### Section 17 — Apportionment of ITC
- If goods/services used for both taxable and exempt supplies → ITC proportional only
- Formula: ITC allowed = (Taxable turnover / Total turnover) × Total ITC

### Section 22 — Registration
- Mandatory if turnover > ₹40 lakh (goods) or ₹20 lakh (services)
- North-east states: ₹20 lakh (goods), ₹10 lakh (services)
- Mandatory regardless of turnover: Interstate suppliers, e-commerce operators, RCM payers

### Section 31 — Tax Invoice
- Must be issued within 30 days of supply (services)
- Must be issued at time of removal (goods)
- Must contain: GSTIN, invoice number, date, HSN/SAC, rate, tax amount

### Section 37 — GSTR-1 (Outward Supplies)
- Monthly: due by 11th of following month (turnover > ₹5 crore)
- Quarterly: due by 13th of following quarter (turnover ≤ ₹5 crore, QRMP scheme)
- Contains: All outward supply invoices, debit/credit notes, exports

### Section 38 — GSTR-2B (Auto-populated ITC)
- Auto-generated from suppliers' GSTR-1
- Available on 14th of every month
- ITC can only be claimed if invoice appears in GSTR-2B

### Section 39 — GSTR-3B (Summary Return)
- Monthly: due by 20th of following month (turnover > ₹5 crore)
- Quarterly: due by 22nd/24th (QRMP scheme, varies by state)
- Contains: Summary of outward supplies, ITC claimed, tax payable

### Section 44 — GSTR-9 (Annual Return)
- Due: 31st December of following financial year
- Mandatory if turnover > ₹2 crore
- Contains: Summary of all monthly returns for the year

### Section 50 — Interest on Late Payment
- Rate: 18% per annum on unpaid tax
- Rate: 24% per annum on excess ITC claimed

### Section 61 — Scrutiny of Returns
- GST officer can scrutinize returns for discrepancies
- Must respond within 30 days of notice

### Section 74 — Fraud/Suppression Cases
- Demand + penalty up to 100% of tax evaded
- 5 years to issue notice (vs 3 years for normal cases)

## GST Audit Rules (Section 65 & 66)

### Departmental Audit (Section 65)
- GST Commissioner can audit any registered person
- Notice must be given 15 days in advance
- Audit must be completed within 3 months (extendable to 6 months)

### Special Audit (Section 66)
- Ordered by Deputy/Assistant Commissioner
- Conducted by CA or CMA nominated by Commissioner
- If value declared is not correct or ITC claimed is incorrect

## Common GST Errors to Flag in Audit

| Error | Impact | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| ITC claimed on blocked items (S.17(5)) | ITC reversal + interest 24% | Critical |
| GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B mismatch | Demand notice from GST dept | Critical |
| GSTR-2B vs Books mismatch | ITC disallowed | Critical |
| Late filing of GSTR-1 | ₹50/day late fee (₹20 for nil) | Review |
| Late filing of GSTR-3B | ₹50/day + 18% interest on tax | Critical |
| Not reversing ITC on exempt supplies | Demand + penalty | Critical |
| B2B invoices not reported in GSTR-1 | Buyer cannot claim ITC | Critical |
| HSN code missing on invoice | Invalid invoice | Review |
| RCM not paid | Demand + 18% interest | Critical |
