# GST Rules — Practical Compliance Rules for Business

**Source:** CGST Rules 2017 (amended up to 2026)

---

## Invoice Rules

### Rule 46 — Tax Invoice Contents (Mandatory Fields)
1. Name, address, GSTIN of supplier
2. Consecutive serial number (unique per FY)
3. Date of issue
4. Name, address, GSTIN of recipient (for registered buyer)
5. HSN code (mandatory if turnover > ₹5 crore)
6. Description of goods/services
7. Quantity and unit
8. Total value
9. Taxable value after discount
10. Rate of tax (CGST/SGST/IGST)
11. Amount of tax
12. Place of supply (for interstate)
13. Signature or digital signature

### Rule 54 — Invoice for Services
- Must be issued within 30 days of supply
- For banks/insurance: 45 days

### Rule 55 — Delivery Challan
- Used when goods move without invoice (e.g. job work, exhibition)
- Must contain: date, serial no, consignor/consignee details, HSN, quantity, value

## ITC Rules

### Rule 36 — Documentary Requirements for ITC
- Original tax invoice or debit note
- Bill of entry (imports)
- Invoice issued by ISD (Input Service Distributor)

### Rule 36(4) — ITC Matching Rule (Critical)
- ITC can be claimed only up to what appears in GSTR-2B
- Excess ITC claimed = reversal required + 24% interest

### Rule 37 — Reversal of ITC (Non-payment to supplier)
- If supplier not paid within 180 days → ITC must be reversed
- Interest: 18% per annum from date of ITC claim
- Can re-claim ITC once payment is made to supplier

### Rule 42 — ITC Reversal for Exempt + Personal Use
- Formula: D1 = (E + N) / T × ITC
  - E = Exempt turnover
  - N = Non-business use value
  - T = Total turnover
  - ITC = Total ITC in period

### Rule 86B — Mandatory Cash Payment
- If turnover > ₹50 lakh → minimum 1% of output tax must be paid in cash
- Exceptions: exporters, inverted duty structure, government entities

## Registration Rules

### Rule 8 — Registration Application
- Apply within 30 days of becoming liable
- Form: GST REG-01
- Documents: PAN, Aadhaar, address proof, bank statement, constitution documents

### Rule 10 — Registration Certificate
- Issued within 3 working days if no query
- GSTIN: 15-digit number (state code + PAN + entity code + check digit)

### Rule 21 — Cancellation of Registration
- Can be cancelled if: no business activity, turnover below threshold, wrong registration
- File Form GST REG-16

## Return Filing Rules

### Rule 59 — GSTR-1 Filing
- Upload all B2B invoices (invoice-wise)
- Upload B2C summary (HSN-wise for invoices > ₹2.5 lakh)
- Export invoices separately
- Debit/credit notes separately

### Rule 61 — GSTR-3B Filing
- Self-assessed summary return
- Pay tax before filing
- Cannot revise GSTR-3B — corrections in next month

### Rule 80 — Annual Return GSTR-9
- Reconcile monthly returns with books
- Declare any additional ITC or tax liability not reported during year

## E-Way Bill Rules

### Rule 138 — When Required
- Movement of goods worth > ₹50,000
- Generated on E-Way Bill portal (ewaybillgst.gov.in)
- Valid for: 1 day per 200 km (can be extended)
- Not required: goods exempted from GST, within 50 km for last-mile delivery

### Rule 138A — Documents to Carry
- Tax invoice / delivery challan
- E-Way Bill number (on vehicle or document)

---

## Refund Rules

### Rule 89 — Refund Application
- File GST RFD-01 within 2 years from relevant date
- Relevant date for exports: date of shipping bill
- Relevant date for excess payment: date of payment

### Rule 96 — Refund on Exports (IGST)
- Automatic refund if shipping bill filed and GSTR-1 matches
- Processed by Customs based on GSTR-1 data

## Penalty Rules

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Not registering despite liability | ₹10,000 or 10% of tax, whichever higher |
| Issuing invoice without supply | ₹10,000 or 100% of tax, whichever higher |
| Fraud/suppression | 100% of tax evaded + imprisonment possible |
| Late filing GSTR-1 | ₹50/day (₹20 for nil return), max ₹10,000 |
| Late filing GSTR-3B | ₹50/day + 18% interest on outstanding tax |
| Wrong ITC claim | 100% penalty + interest 24% |
