# GST FAQs — Common Questions in Audit & Compliance

---

## Registration FAQs

**Q: When is GST registration mandatory?**
- Goods: Turnover > ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for special category states)
- Services: Turnover > ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for special category states)
- Always mandatory: interstate suppliers, e-commerce sellers, RCM payers, casual taxable persons

**Q: Can a person have multiple GSTINs?**
- Yes — one GSTIN per state per PAN
- If business in 3 states → 3 GSTINs

**Q: What is the penalty for not registering?**
- ₹10,000 or 10% of tax due, whichever is higher

## ITC FAQs

**Q: Can ITC be claimed on goods used for personal purpose?**
- No — ITC only for business purpose

**Q: Supplier filed GSTR-1 but ITC not showing in GSTR-2B — can I claim?**
- No — ITC allowed only if it appears in GSTR-2B (Rule 36(4))
- Follow up with supplier to correct their return

**Q: ITC claimed but supplier didn't pay tax to government — what happens?**
- GST department can demand recovery from buyer
- Buyer must pay back ITC + 18% interest

**Q: Can ITC be carried forward to next year?**
- Yes — ITC balance in electronic credit ledger carries forward
- No time limit for utilisation

**Q: ITC on capital goods — how to claim?**
- Full ITC in year of purchase (not depreciated like in Income Tax)
- If capital good sold within 5 years → ITC reversal required

## Invoice FAQs

**Q: Buyer's GSTIN not mentioned on invoice — can ITC be claimed?**
- No — for B2B ITC, GSTIN of buyer is mandatory on invoice

**Q: Can invoice be amended after filing GSTR-1?**
- Yes — through debit note or credit note
- Amendment in GSTR-1 allowed up to September of next FY or annual return date

**Q: What if invoice date and supply date are different?**
- Tax liability arises on date of supply (not invoice date)
- Invoice must be issued within 30 days of supply (services)

## Return Filing FAQs

**Q: Filed GSTR-3B with wrong figures — how to correct?**
- Cannot revise GSTR-3B
- Correct in next month's GSTR-3B (Table 9 — amendments)
- Pay differential tax + interest if underpaid

**Q: Forgot to claim ITC in monthly return — deadline?**
- Can claim ITC up to: earlier of 30 November of next FY or date of filing annual return
- After that — ITC lapses permanently

**Q: GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B figures don't match — problem?**
- Yes — system generates ASMT-10 notice automatically
- Must respond with explanation or pay differential tax

## Reconciliation FAQs

**Q: What is GSTR-2A vs GSTR-2B difference?**
- GSTR-2A: Dynamic, changes as supplier files returns
- GSTR-2B: Static, generated on 14th every month — use THIS for ITC claims

**Q: Books show ₹1,00,000 ITC but GSTR-2B shows ₹90,000 — what to do?**
1. Check if missing invoices are from suppliers who haven't filed GSTR-1
2. Follow up with those suppliers
3. Claim only ₹90,000 in GSTR-3B
4. When supplier files, the remaining ₹10,000 appears next month

**Q: Annual return GSTR-9 shows more tax than monthly returns — do I pay?**
- Yes — pay differential tax in DRC-03 (voluntary payment)
- Interest @ 18% applicable from original due date

## RCM (Reverse Charge) FAQs

**Q: What supplies attract RCM?**
- Services from unregistered persons (in specified categories)
- Legal services by advocate to business
- Director's remuneration
- Import of services
- GTA (Goods Transport Agency) services
- Security services

**Q: Can ITC be claimed on RCM paid?**
- Yes — same month after payment (not on accrual)

**Q: Is RCM applicable on purchases from unregistered dealers?**
- Only for specified categories (not all purchases)
- For goods — mostly not applicable except specific items

## Exports FAQs

**Q: What are the options for export under GST?**
1. Export under Bond/LUT — supply without paying IGST, claim refund of ITC
2. Export with payment of IGST — pay IGST, claim refund

**Q: What is LUT?**
- Letter of Undertaking — file on GST portal before exporting
- Renew every financial year
- Allows export without paying IGST
