## Section 194C — Contractor Payments (Most Common)

**Who deducts:** Any person paying to resident contractor
**Threshold:** ₹30,000 per transaction OR ₹1,00,000 aggregate in FY
**Rate:** 1% (individual/HUF), 2% (others)

**What is a "contract":**
- Transport contracts
- Service contracts
- Advertising contracts
- Labour contracts
- Manufacturing contracts
- Supply contracts

**CBDT Circular 715 (1995):** Payment to advertising agency = 194C (not 194J)
**CBDT Circular 2016-04:** Clarified transport contracts — owner-operators with own vehicles exempt if they give PAN

**When NOT 194C:**
- Professional services → 194J
- Rent → 194I
- Commission → 194H

## Section 194J — Professional/Technical Services

**Who deducts:** Any person (except individual/HUF below audit limit)
**Threshold:** ₹30,000 per payee per FY
**Rate:** 10% (professional fees), 2% (technical services, call centres)

**Professional services (10%):**
- Legal services (advocate)
- Medical services (doctor)
- Engineering services
- Architectural services
- Accountancy services (CA)
- Technical consultancy
- Film artists

**Technical services (2%):**
- Managerial services
- IT services
- Call centre services
- Assembly/processing services

**Director's remuneration (not salary):** 194J @ 10%

## Section 192 — Salary TDS

**Who deducts:** Employer
**When:** At time of payment
**Rate:** As per applicable income tax slab

**Calculation:**
1. Gross salary (including all allowances, perquisites)
2. Less: HRA exemption (Section 10(13A))
3. Less: Leave travel allowance (Section 10(5)) — twice in 4 years
4. Less: Standard deduction ₹75,000 (FY 2025-26)
5. Less: Chapter VIA deductions (80C, 80D etc.) — if employee declares
6. = Net taxable salary
7. Calculate tax on net taxable salary at slab rates
8. Less: Rebate under 87A (if income < ₹12 lakh)
9. Add: Health and Education Cess 4%
10. = Annual tax liability → deduct monthly

**Form 16:** Must be issued by 15 June after FY end
**Form 12BB:** Employee submits to employer with investment declarations

## TDS — Due Dates

| Month | TDS Deposit Due Date |
|---|---|
| April to February | 7th of next month |
| March | 30th April |

**TDS Returns (Quarterly):**
| Quarter | Period | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April-June | 31st July |
| Q2 | July-September | 31st October |
| Q3 | October-December | 31st January |
| Q4 | January-March | 31st May |

## TDS — Interest and Penalties

| Default | Interest/Penalty |
|---|---|
| Non-deduction of TDS | 1% per month from deduction date to actual deduction |
| Non-deposit of TDS | 1.5% per month from deduction date to deposit date |
| Late filing of TDS return | ₹200 per day (Section 234E) |
| Non-filing of TDS return | ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000 (Section 271H) |
| Wrong PAN in TDS return | 20% TDS instead of applicable rate |

## Lower TDS / NIL TDS Certificate

**Section 197:** Recipient can apply to AO for lower deduction certificate
- Form 13 — Application
- AO issues certificate specifying lower rate
- Deductor must deduct at that lower rate

**Section 206AB:** If recipient hasn't filed ITR for 2 years and TDS > ₹50,000 → double TDS rate

---

## Form 26AS and AIS

**Form 26AS:** Tax credit statement showing all TDS deducted against your PAN
- Available on Income Tax portal (incometaxindia.gov.in)
- Reconcile with books before filing ITR

**AIS (Annual Information Statement):** More detailed — includes:
- TDS/TCS
- Interest income
- Dividend
- Securities transactions
- Foreign remittances
- Rent received
