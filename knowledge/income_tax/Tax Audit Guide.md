# Tax Audit Guide — Section 44AB Income Tax Act

**Source:** Income Tax Act 1961 (applicable up to FY 2025-26)
**Note:** New Income Tax Act 2025 effective from FY 2026-27 — section numbers change but provisions same

---

## Who Needs Tax Audit?

| Category | Threshold | Section |
|---|---|---|
| Business (normal) | Turnover > ₹1 crore | 44AB(a) |
| Business (cash transactions ≤ 5%) | Turnover > ₹10 crore | 44AB(a) proviso |
| Profession | Gross receipts > ₹50 lakh | 44AB(b) |
| Presumptive business (44AD) opting out | Any income | 44AB(e) |
| Presumptive profession (44ADA) opting out | Any income | 44AB(f) |

**Due date:** 30 September of assessment year (e.g. for FY 2025-26 → 30 Sep 2026)
**Penalty for non-compliance:** 0.5% of turnover or ₹1,50,000 — whichever is lower (Section 271B)

## Tax Audit Report — Form 3CA/3CB/3CD

### Form 3CA — When accounts are audited under another law (Companies Act)
### Form 3CB — When accounts are NOT audited under another law (proprietorship, partnership)

## Form 3CD — Key Clauses

### Clause 4 — Whether books maintained
- Books of account maintained at which address
- Nature of books: Cash book, ledger, journal, etc.

### Clause 9 — Method of Accounting
- Cash basis or Mercantile basis
- Any change in method during the year?

### Clause 12 — Profits and Gains from Business
- Any amount credited to P&L but not disclosed as income?

### Clause 13 — Method of Valuation of Stock
- Opening stock and closing stock valuation method
- Any change in valuation method?

### Clause 14 — Depreciation (Section 32)
- Block-wise depreciation as per IT Act rates
- WDV of each block at start and end
- Additions and deletions during year
- Depreciation claimed

**Key IT Depreciation Rates:**
| Asset | Rate |
|---|---|
| Buildings (residential) | 5% |
| Buildings (commercial) | 10% |
| Plant & Machinery (general) | 15% |
| Computers & software | 40% |
| Vehicles | 15% |
| Furniture | 10% |
| Intangibles (patent, trademark) | 25% |

### Clause 16 — Amounts Debited to P&L (Disallowances)

**Section 40A(3) — Cash payments > ₹10,000:**
- Any single payment > ₹10,000 in cash to a person in a day → DISALLOWED
- Exception: payments to RBI, banks, government, villages without banking
- Audit must list all such payments

**Section 40(a)(ia) — TDS not deducted:**
- If TDS not deducted on payment → 30% of expense disallowed
- If TDS deducted but not deposited → 30% disallowed

**Section 36(1)(va) — Employee PF/ESI:**
- Employee's contribution to PF/ESI must be deposited BEFORE due date of return
- If deposited late → DISALLOWED entirely (even by 1 day)

### Clause 17 — Amounts Not Credited to P&L
- Personal expenses taken from business account?
- Capital receipts credited to revenue?

### Clause 18 — Depreciation
- WDV method (IT Act) vs SLM (Companies Act) — differences explained here
- Deferred tax implications

### Clause 19 — Deductions under Section 80
- 80C (LIC, PPF, ELSS) — for proprietors
- 80D (health insurance)
- 80G (donations)
- 80IC/80IE (area-based deductions)
- 80P (cooperative societies)

### Clause 21 — Amounts Inadmissible

**Section 40(b) — Partner's Remuneration:**
- Remuneration to working partners allowed only if:
  - Specified in partnership deed
  - Within limits: ₹3 lakh or 90% of book profit (first ₹3 lakh of profit), 60% thereafter

**Section 40(b) — Interest to Partners:**
- Maximum 12% per annum allowed
- Excess interest disallowed

### Clause 26 — Section 269SS — Loans/Deposits in Cash
- No person should accept loan/deposit > ₹20,000 in cash
- Penalty: Equal amount of loan (Section 271D)
- Audit must report all violations

### Clause 27 — Section 269T — Repayment of Loans in Cash
- No repayment of loan > ₹20,000 in cash
- Penalty: Equal amount (Section 271E)

### Clause 31 — TDS/TCS Details
- All TDS deducted and deposited
- Any TDS default (non-deduction or late deposit)

### Clause 34 — TDS Compliance
- Whether TDS returns filed on time
- Any TDS demand pending

## Common Tax Audit Disallowances — Checklist

| Item | Section | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Cash payment > ₹10,000 | 40A(3) | 100% disallowed |
| TDS not deducted on expense | 40(a)(ia) | 30% disallowed |
| Employee PF/ESI deposited late | 36(1)(va) | 100% disallowed |
| Partner's salary above limit | 40(b) | Excess disallowed |
| Partner's interest above 12% | 40(b) | Excess disallowed |
| Penalty paid for law violation | 37 | 100% disallowed |
| Personal expense in P&L | 37 | 100% disallowed |
| Cash loan received > ₹20,000 | 269SS | Penalty + disallowed |
| Cash loan repaid > ₹20,000 | 269T | Penalty + disallowed |
| Donation to unregistered trust | 80G | Not deductible |

## Presumptive Taxation

### Section 44AD — Business
- For turnover up to ₹3 crore (if digital receipts ≥ 95%)
- Deemed profit: 8% of turnover (6% if digital)
- No books of account required
- Cannot claim any deductions separately

### Section 44ADA — Profession
- For gross receipts up to ₹75 lakh
- Deemed profit: 50% of gross receipts
- Professions: Doctor, lawyer, architect, CA, engineer, interior designer, technical consultant

### Section 44AE — Transport Business
- Per vehicle per month: ₹1,000 per ton (heavy goods) or ₹7,500 (other)
- Cannot own more than 10 goods vehicles
