# ITR Filing Guide — Business & Profession

---

## Which ITR Form to Use?

| Entity | Form | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Individual — salary only | ITR-1 (Sahaj) | Income < ₹50 lakh, no business |
| Individual — capital gains | ITR-2 | No business income |
| Individual/HUF — business/profession | ITR-3 | Regular books of accounts |
| Individual/HUF — presumptive income | ITR-4 (Sugam) | 44AD/44ADA/44AE |
| Partnership firm/LLP | ITR-5 | |
| Company | ITR-6 | Not claiming 11/12 exemption |
| Trust/charity | ITR-7 | |

## ITR-3 — Business/Profession (Most Common for SMBs)

### What to fill:
1. **Personal Information** — PAN, Aadhaar, address, bank details
2. **Nature of Business** — Business code (as per schedule)
3. **Balance Sheet** — Assets and Liabilities as of 31 March
4. **P&L Account** — Income and Expenditure for the year
5. **Tax Computation** — Disallowances added back, deductions claimed
6. **TDS/TCS Credits** — Match with Form 26AS
7. **Tax Paid** — Advance tax, self-assessment tax, TDS
8. **Verification** — Sign with DSC or EVC

## Advance Tax — Section 207/208

**Who must pay:** If tax liability > ₹10,000 (after TDS credit)
**Exception:** Senior citizens (60+) with no business income

| Installment | Due Date | Cumulative % |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | 15 June | 15% |
| 2nd | 15 September | 45% |
| 3rd | 15 December | 75% |
| 4th | 15 March | 100% |

**Interest for short payment:** 1% per month (Section 234C)
**Interest for no advance tax:** 1% per month (Section 234B)

## Capital Gains — Quick Reference

### Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)
- Listed equity shares/MF held < 1 year: 20% (changed from 15% — Budget 2024)
- Other assets held < 2 years (land, building): as per slab
- Other assets held < 3 years (debt MF, gold): as per slab

### Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)
- Listed equity/MF held > 1 year: 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh (changed Budget 2024)
- Land/building held > 2 years: 12.5% without indexation (changed Budget 2024)
- Debt MF held > 3 years: as per slab (indexation removed)

### Indexation — Cost Inflation Index (CII)
- CII for FY 2025-26: 376
- Indexed cost = (Cost × CII of sale year) / (CII of purchase year)
- **Note:** Indexation benefit removed for property from July 2024 in most cases

## Deductions Under Chapter VIA

| Section | What | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| 80C | LIC, PPF, ELSS, home loan principal, tuition fees | ₹1,50,000 |

## New vs Old Tax Regime (FY 2025-26)

### Old Regime — with deductions
| Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | Nil |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

### New Regime (default from FY 2023-24)
| Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹3,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹3,00,001 – ₹7,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹7,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹10,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹15,00,000 | 30% |

**Rebate 87A:** Tax nil if income ≤ ₹12,00,000 (new regime) / ₹5,00,000 (old regime)
**Surcharge and Cess:** Add 4% health & education cess on tax amount

## Common ITR Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not declaring all bank accounts
2. Not matching TDS with Form 26AS/AIS
3. Forgetting to report exempt income (LTA, gratuity, PF)
4. Wrong ITR form selection
5. Not paying advance tax → interest u/s 234B/C
6. Claiming deductions not eligible under new regime
7. Not e-verifying return within 30 days
8. Not reporting foreign assets (Schedule FA)
9. Not carrying forward losses (need to file before due date)
10. Cash deposits not explained in return
